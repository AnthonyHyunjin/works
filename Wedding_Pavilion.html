wedding pavilion

 

Carnegie Mellon University - Year 1 Semester 1

Professors: William Douglas Cooper, Gerard Damiani, Stephen Quick

Oct. 2016 - Dec. 2016

 
...
 

drawings

 

Using three rectangular planes that meet at a singular perpendicular point, a cube that houses the planes, and the site around the cube, a wedding pavilion is to be designed and modeled with the representation of solving space making problems.

 

The drawings depict one site plan, section perspective, and a drafted section, elevation, and paraline, all in 1'=1/4" scale. The site context include property lines to the north and south, a road to the east and Lake Erie to the west.

...
 

model

 

In order to fully understand the representation of space making, a crafted model made completely out of basswood and plywoos was cut and assembled at a 1'=1/4" scale.

 

These models were also made to practice the transferring of drawing, drafting and designing to constructing, crafting and assembling.
